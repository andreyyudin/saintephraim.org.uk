COVID-19 (Coronavirus)

Dear brothers and sisters,

Please find the English and Russian versions of a Statement issued by His Grace Bishop Matthew of Sourozh, about the COVID-19 (Coronavirus) outbreak. Please also find the message of His Grace Metropolitan Anthony of Korsun and Western Europe in English and in Russian.

In view of the Bishop’s and Metropolitan's recommendations:

1. Until further notice, individual confessions before services are suspended. Instead they will now be replaced by a rite of General Confession each Saturday evening, immediately after Vespers or Vigil. For the moment, General Confession will be the norm in the parish. Those who feel the need for individual confession, should discuss this with me, with a view to meeting at some point during the week, at the Church.

2. We shall have individual paper cups available by the weekend for use with the ‘zapifka’ following reception of Holy Communion.

3. Parents are encouraged to bring a snack for their children to eat after the service but, with immediate effect, there will be no coffee or other refreshments served after the Divine Liturgy on Sunday

4. We must continue in fervent prayer for all those who are suffering from the Coronavirus and for the pandemic to come to an early end.

With my love in Christ,
Fr Raphael

Welcome to our website!


We are an Orthodox Parish in Cambridge, UK of the Diocese of Sourozh - the diocese of the Russian Orthodox Church which covers the British Isles. Our parish was founded in 1973 by Metropolitan Anthony of Sourozh. We celebrate Great Vespers or Vigil each Saturday at 17:00 and the Divine Liturgy each Sunday at 08:00. Services are also celebrated on the great feasts of the Orthodox Church.

The Parish

We are an international Orthodox Parish. The services in the parish are, for the most part in English. Our parishioners come from Russia, Romania, Bulgaria, Serbia, Greece, Bulgaria and Cyprus with others, mainly converts, from the UK, Germany, Switzerland, Zimbabwe, Australia, Hong Kong, South Africa and the USA. Each year several University students become part of the parish during their studies in Cambridge. From the 30 people who worshipped at the Divine Liturgy in 2001, on most Sundays we now have some 120 people, about 20% being under the age of eight!
